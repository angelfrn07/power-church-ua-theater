## Project Title

**Commercial Kitchen and Food Pantry Buildout — East Amarillo Multi-Use Community Facility**

## Amount Requested

**$50,000**

This is a one-time, non-recurring capital request for a specific component of a larger renovation. Funds will be used solely for the kitchen and food-pantry buildout, with no operating-cost component included.

## Project Summary (one paragraph)

Power Church is in the process of acquiring the former United Artists movie theater at 8275 W Amarillo Boulevard — an 83,422-square-foot facility on ten acres — to expand our weekly food-pantry partnership with the High Plains Food Bank and to bring food-insecurity relief to substantially more Amarillo families. We respectfully request $50,000 from the Mary E. Bivins Foundation toward the renovation of the building's commercial kitchen and food-pantry distribution area: a one-time, fully bounded capital expense within a larger renovation. With this kitchen and pantry buildout in place, our food-pantry capacity will scale from approximately 50 families per week (our current ceiling) to 150–200 families per week — a three-to-four-fold expansion of our service to families experiencing food insecurity in East Amarillo.

## The Need

Food insecurity remains one of the most persistent challenges facing East Amarillo families. Power Church has operated a weekly Sunday food pantry since 2017 in partnership with the High Plains Food Bank, distributing groceries to approximately fifty households per week. Demand has consistently exceeded what our current SE 10th Avenue building can hold. We have routinely turned families away because the existing kitchen, storage, and parking infrastructure cannot support the volume our community needs. The challenge is not commitment, partnership, or volunteer capacity — those are in place. The challenge is space, refrigeration, and the food-handling infrastructure required to safely serve hundreds of families weekly.

## The Opportunity

The 8275 W Amarillo Boulevard facility was originally built with a commercial-grade kitchen footprint to support a full-service movie theater concession. With approximately $50,000 in targeted renovation, we can convert this kitchen into a community food-pantry production and distribution space that meets all Texas Department of State Health Services food-handling standards. The lobby — designed to circulate hundreds of patrons at a time — becomes a dignified pantry distribution area with shopping-style selection rather than the cramped curbside setup our current building forces.

## Christian Mission and the Bivins Foundation's Founding Vision

Mary E. Bivins's lifelong faith and her lifelong commitment to the Texas Panhandle shaped this Foundation. Power Church's food-pantry ministry is the most direct expression we know of one of the simplest commands in the Christian faith — feed the hungry. We do this every Sunday now, and the new facility will let us do it for many more families. The Bivins Foundation's investment will be the catalyst that converts a fully built but unused commercial kitchen into one of the largest church-operated food-pantry distribution centers in the Texas Panhandle.

## Power Church's Track Record in East Amarillo

Power Church has been an active and consistent presence in the Amarillo community for over a decade. Our documented community work, sourced to local Amarillo media coverage from 2017 through 2026, includes:

- **Weekly Sunday food pantry** in partnership with the High Plains Food Bank, ongoing since 2017
- **Annual Barrio Bash** community block party — 3,000+ attendees in 2017, partnership with the City of Amarillo, Potter County, and the Amarillo Police Department
- **Barrio Community Watch** crime-prevention program launched with APD in 2017 and ongoing
- **Faith Community Summit** — hosted the Amarillo Domestic Violence Coalition's annual training in October 2022
- **Healing Prayer Walk** — March 2026 community response to neighborhood violence
- **Volunteer support** at the annual Back-to-School Barrio Bash & Horseshoe Tournament at Alamo Park
- **Amarillo Barrio Community Development** — companion 501(c)(3) (EIN 47-4527312, founded 2015) that Pastor Manny established to extend the church's anti-poverty work

This track record — and Power Church's banking relationship with Amarillo National Bank for the new facility — give the Foundation confidence that funded capital will produce real, durable community impact.
