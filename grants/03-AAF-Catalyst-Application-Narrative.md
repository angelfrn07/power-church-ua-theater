## Project Summary (one paragraph)

Amarillo Barrio Community Development requests $20,000 to launch foundational programming at a newly acquired multi-use community facility at 8275 W Amarillo Boulevard. The facility — formerly the United Artists movie theater — is being acquired and renovated by our partner Power Church. Beginning in late 2026, ABCD will operate three core community programs from the new building: (1) an expanded weekly food pantry in partnership with the High Plains Food Bank, (2) a children's after-school program for low-income East Amarillo families, and (3) a free family-resources hub offering case management and referrals. This grant will fund the supplies, training, equipment, and outreach materials required to bring the three programs online during the first twelve months of operation.

## The Need

East Amarillo and the surrounding Barrio neighborhood remain one of Amarillo's most under-resourced areas. Residents face documented challenges around food insecurity, gaps in affordable child care, and uneven access to family-support services. ABCD has worked alongside this community since 2015. Power Church, our partner congregation, has hosted a weekly food pantry since 2017 in collaboration with the High Plains Food Bank — serving approximately 50 families per week in early years and reaching as many as 200 families per distribution event in recent years. The current facility at 1344 SE 10th Avenue, however, is at the operational ceiling of what its space, kitchen, and parking can support. Demand consistently exceeds capacity.

## The Opportunity

Power Church is in the process of acquiring 8275 W Amarillo Boulevard, an 83,422-square-foot multi-use building on a 10-acre lot with substantial commercial-grade infrastructure (commercial kitchen, large lobby, classroom-suitable rooms, sanctuary, and 629-space parking lot). Once renovation is complete, this facility will multiply ABCD's program capacity and open new programming options that the SE 10th Avenue building cannot support. Catalyst grant funds will allow ABCD to launch foundational community programming the moment the building is operational — without delay caused by gaps in startup capital.

## Three Programs Funded by This Request

1. **Expanded Food Pantry — High Plains Food Bank Partnership.** Supplies, intake materials, transportation reimbursement for volunteer drivers, and signage. Expected to scale weekly distribution from approximately 50 families to 150–200 families per week with the new facility's commercial kitchen and larger lobby.

2. **Children's After-School Program.** Curriculum supplies, snack/meal program startup, basic furnishings for two classroom rooms, and outreach materials to recruit Amarillo ISD partner schools. Expected to serve approximately 40 children per afternoon, focused on East Amarillo families.

3. **Family Resources Hub.** Case-management intake materials, training for two part-time staff or volunteer case managers, partnership-development outreach to existing Amarillo nonprofits (Catholic Charities, Family Support Services, Hope and Healing Place, etc.), and operating supplies for a small intake office. Expected to provide referrals and case management for an estimated 100 East Amarillo families per year in Year 1.

## Why ABCD is the Right Applicant

ABCD has worked alongside East Amarillo for ten years, with documented community partnerships across municipal government, law enforcement, the High Plains Food Bank, and the broader Amarillo nonprofit network. The organization holds an active 501(c)(3) public-charity status and has the operational infrastructure — board governance, mission alignment, partner relationships, and on-the-ground knowledge of East Amarillo families — required to launch this programming responsibly and report against funder expectations.

## Sustainability Plan

After the twelve-month Catalyst-funded launch period, ABCD will sustain the three programs through a combination of: (a) program-specific federal grants the organization will pursue (including HUD Section 4 Capacity Building, USDA Community Facilities, and DOJ Project Safe Neighborhoods), (b) continuing AAF Discretionary cycles, (c) Walmart Spark Good and Home Depot Foundation cycles, and (d) ABCD's growing donor base. Power Church has committed to providing the facility space at no cost to ABCD for the foreseeable future, eliminating the largest sustaining cost typical of nonprofit programming.
