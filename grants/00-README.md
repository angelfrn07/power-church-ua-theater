## Why this order

The pre-submission consultation calls are the most leveraged hours in the entire process. They:

1. **Surface "we don't fund that" before any application work is wasted.** If Bivins says they don't fund church capital projects, Pastor Manny pivots to a different framing or to a different funder rather than wasting hours on the application.
2. **Let the grants officer pre-shape the application.** Both AAF and Bivins explicitly recommend pre-submission contact. Funders who help shape an application typically advocate for it later — the consultation is also a relationship-build.
3. **Catch program-fit nuances.** For Bivins, the question of which renovation component to lead with (kitchen, daycare, youth wing) is the kind of guidance only Tracy Selle can give. Don't guess; ask.

## After submission — Walmart Spark Good (third TOP grant)

Walmart Spark Good Cycle 2 closes July 15, 2026 (over two months out). The application is dramatically simpler than AAF or Bivins. Pastor Manny should:

1. Register Power Church AND Amarillo Barrio Community Development on `walmart.com/nonprofits` this week (Deed verification takes 7–10 days).
2. Once verified, identify all five+ Amarillo Walmart and Sam's Club store managers.
3. Apply to each store individually for a $250–$5,000 grant — Walmart explicitly allows multi-store stacking.

The Walmart narrative re-uses 90% of the AAF Catalyst language but emphasizes the food-pantry / High Plains Food Bank partnership, since Walmart Spark Good prioritizes hunger and community-anchor partners.
